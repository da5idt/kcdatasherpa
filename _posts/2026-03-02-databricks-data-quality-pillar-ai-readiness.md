I came across an article titled **“Databricks Data Quality: A Pillar of AI Readiness”**, and it reinforces something most experienced data teams already know: data quality is expensive, ambiguous, and absolutely necessary.

It costs compute to monitor, engineering time to implement, and real effort to define what “quality” even means across different datasets.

Finance, operations, and sales all define quality differently, and without clarity, initiatives stall.

The hard truth is that quality work rarely wins over feature delivery.

It slows teams down in the short term and introduces operational overhead. But ignoring it creates far more expensive problems later.

Weak quality practices erode trust, increase firefighting, and make AI initiatives fragile at best.

Done right, data quality is not a nice-to-have layer on top of a platform. It is foundational infrastructure.

And it requires leadership discipline to prioritize it when it is inconvenient, not just when something breaks.
